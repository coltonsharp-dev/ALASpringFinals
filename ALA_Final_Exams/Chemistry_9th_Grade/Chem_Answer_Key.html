SECTION A · 17 POINTS · 1 POINT EACH
Vocabulary — Answer Key
ATOM
Smallest particle of an element retaining its chemical properties.
ELEMENT
Pure substance of one type of atom; cannot be chemically broken down further.
COMPOUND
Pure substance of 2+ elements chemically bonded in a fixed ratio.
MIXTURE
2+ substances NOT chemically combined; each retains own properties.
PROTON
Positive (+) subatomic particle in the nucleus. Count = atomic number.
NEUTRON
Neutral subatomic particle in the nucleus. Changing neutrons → isotopes.
ELECTRON
Negative (−) subatomic particle in orbitals around the nucleus.
ATOMIC NUMBER
Number of protons in the nucleus. Unique to each element; never changes.
MASS NUMBER
Protons + Neutrons in the nucleus.
ISOTOPE
Same element (same protons), different neutron count, different mass number.
ION
Atom that gained electrons (anion, −) or lost electrons (cation, +).
VALENCE ELECTRONS
Outermost electrons; responsible for chemical bonding.
COVALENT BOND
Bond formed by SHARING electrons; usually between nonmetals.
IONIC BOND
Bond formed by TRANSFERRING electrons; metal to nonmetal.
MOLE
SI unit for amount; 1 mol = 6.022 × 10²³ particles (Avogadro's number).
REACTANT
Starting substance consumed in a reaction; LEFT side of equation.
PRODUCT
New substance formed in a reaction; RIGHT side of equation.
SECTION B · 20 POINTS · 1 POINT EACH
Multiple Choice — Answer Key with Rationale
L01 · MATTER & MEASUREMENT
Q18 · Physical vs. Chemical Change
D. Ice melting
Which of the following is an example of a physical change?
CORRECT ANSWER
D. Ice melting into liquid water
Physical change: only the state of matter changes (solid → liquid). H₂O remains H₂O. No new substances are formed. Rusting (A), burning (B), curdling (C), rotting (E), and the baking soda reaction (F) all produce new chemical substances — those are chemical changes.
Q19 · Classification of Matter
C. Homogeneous mixture
Salt water (NaCl dissolved in H₂O) is best classified as a...
CORRECT ANSWER
C. Homogeneous mixture
Salt water contains two substances not chemically combined (NaCl + H₂O). The salt can be recovered by evaporation. Composition is uniform throughout (homogeneous). It is NOT a pure compound because the ratio of salt to water can vary.
Q20 · Density Calculation ★
E. 9.0 g/cm³
A metal sample: mass = 45.0 g, volume = 5.0 cm³. Density?
DENSITY CALCULATION — FULL WORK
Formula: D = m ÷ v
Values: m = 45.0 g | v = 5.0 cm³
Solve: D = 45.0 ÷ 5.0
Answer: D = 9.0 g/cm³
Common errors: dividing volume by mass (gives 0.11) or multiplying (gives 225). Always identify the formula first: D = m/v.
L02 · ATOMIC STRUCTURE
Q21 · Location of Atomic Mass
E. In the nucleus
Where is the majority of an atom's mass located?
CORRECT ANSWER
E. In the nucleus
Protons (~1 amu each) and neutrons (~1 amu each) are both in the nucleus. Electrons are ~1/1836 the mass of a proton — negligible. Rutherford's gold-foil experiment confirmed that nearly all mass is concentrated in a tiny, dense nucleus.
Q22 · Mass Number Calculation ★
D. 35
An atom has 17 protons and 18 neutrons. Mass number?
MASS NUMBER CALCULATION — FULL WORK
Formula: Mass Number = Protons + Neutrons
Values: Protons = 17 | Neutrons = 18
Solve: 17 + 18
Answer: Mass Number = 35 (Chlorine-35, ³⁵Cl)
Note: atomic number (17) = element identity (Chlorine). Mass number (35) = this specific isotope.
Q23 · Element Identity
C. Proton
Which subatomic particle determines the identity of an atom?
CORRECT ANSWER
C. Proton (via atomic number)
Change proton count → different element. Change neutrons → isotope of the SAME element. Change electrons → ion of the SAME element. The atomic number (proton count) is the defining characteristic.
L03 · PERIODIC TABLE
Q24 · Groups vs. Periods
F. Chemical properties
Elements in the same group share similar...
CORRECT ANSWER
F. Chemical properties
Same group = same number of valence electrons = similar chemical behavior. Example: Group 1 (Li, Na, K) all have 1 valence electron and all react vigorously with water. Periods share the same number of electron shells, not valence electrons.
Q25 · Noble Gases
F. Argon (Ar)
Which element is a noble gas?
CORRECT ANSWER
F. Argon (Ar) — Group 18
Noble gases (Group 18): He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn. Full valence shells = chemically stable/unreactive. Distractors: N (Group 15), O (Group 16), F (Group 17/halogen), Cl (Group 17/halogen), Na (Group 1/alkali metal).
Q26 · Periodic Trends
E. Atomic number
Moving left to right across Period 3, which property steadily increases?
CORRECT ANSWER
E. Atomic number
Atomic number increases by exactly 1 for each element across any period — that is how the table is organized. Atomic radius DECREASES across a period (more protons pull electrons closer). Metallic character also DECREASES moving right.
L04 · CHEMICAL BONDING
Q27 · Ionic vs. Covalent
F. Ionic bond
Which bond involves the complete TRANSFER of electrons?
CORRECT ANSWER
F. Ionic bond
Ionic bond: electrons fully transferred from metal (loses e⁻ → cation) to nonmetal (gains e⁻ → anion). Na⁺ and Cl⁻ attract and form NaCl. Covalent bond: electrons are SHARED (not transferred).
Q28 · Valence Electrons of Carbon
E. 4
How many valence electrons does carbon (Group 14) have?
CORRECT ANSWER
E. 4 valence electrons
For main-group elements, the group number gives the valence electron count: Group 14 = 4 valence electrons. Carbon can form 4 bonds — fundamental to all organic chemistry and life.
Q29 · Molecular Shape of Water
C. Bent (V-shaped)
H₂O has two lone pairs on O. What molecular shape results?
CORRECT ANSWER
C. Bent (V-shaped) — ~104.5° bond angle
Oxygen has 4 electron groups (2 bonds + 2 lone pairs) → tetrahedral electron geometry. Molecular shape only counts ATOMS, giving bent. The lone pairs push H atoms closer together. This bent shape + electronegativity difference = polar molecule = why water has surface tension, high boiling point, etc.
L05 · CHEMICAL REACTIONS
Q30 · Law of Conservation of Mass
F. Mass & number of atoms
What is always conserved in a chemical reaction?
CORRECT ANSWER
F. Total mass and total number of each type of atom
This is the Law of Conservation of Mass (Lavoisier, 1789). Matter is neither created nor destroyed. This is exactly why we balance chemical equations — the number and type of atoms on the left must equal the right.
Q31 · Reaction Types
F. 2H₂O₂ → 2H₂O + O₂
Which represents a decomposition reaction?
CORRECT ANSWER
F. 2H₂O₂ → 2H₂O + O₂ (hydrogen peroxide decomposing)
Decomposition: ONE compound → TWO or more products. Reaction type reference: A = synthesis, B = combustion, C = single-replacement, D = double-replacement (neutralization), E = synthesis.
Q32 · Coefficients vs. Subscripts
E. Moles / molecules
What do coefficients in a balanced equation represent?
CORRECT ANSWER
E. The number of moles (or molecules) of each substance
COEFFICIENT (large, in front): number of moles/molecules. SUBSCRIPT (small, inside formula): number of atoms in one molecule. NEVER change subscripts to balance — that changes the substance itself (e.g., H₂O → H₃O changes the compound).
L06 · STOICHIOMETRY
Q33 · Avogadro's Number
D. 1 mole of any substance
6.022 × 10²³ represents the number of particles in exactly...
CORRECT ANSWER
D. 1 mole of any substance
By definition: 1 mole = 6.022 × 10²³ representative particles. This applies to atoms, molecules, ions, formula units, etc. Avogadro's number was chosen so that atomic mass in amu = molar mass in g/mol.
Q34 · Molar Mass of H₂O ★
F. 18.0 g/mol
Molar mass of H₂O? (H = 1.0 g/mol, O = 16.0 g/mol)
MOLAR MASS — H₂O — FULL WORK
Formula: MM = Σ (# atoms × atomic mass)
Hydrogen: 2 atoms × 1.0 g/mol = 2.0 g/mol
Oxygen: 1 atom × 16.0 g/mol = 16.0 g/mol
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Total MM = 18.0 g/mol
Common error: students add all atomic masses without multiplying by count. 2 H atoms × 1.0 = 2.0, NOT just 1.0.
Q35 · Moles from Mass ★
F. 2.0 mol
How many moles in 36.0 g of water? (MM = 18.0 g/mol)
MOLES CALCULATION — FULL WORK
Formula: n = m ÷ M
n = moles | m = mass (g) | M = molar mass (g/mol)
Values: m = 36.0 g | M = 18.0 g/mol
Solve: n = 36.0 ÷ 18.0
Answer: n = 2.0 mol
Intuition check: if 1 mol = 18 g, then 36 g = 2 mol. Like: if 1 dozen = 12 eggs, 24 eggs = 2 dozen.
L07 · STATES OF MATTER & SOLUTIONS
Q36 · States of Matter
E. Liquid
Which state has a definite volume but no definite shape?
CORRECT ANSWER
E. Liquid
Solid: definite shape + definite volume (particles locked). Liquid: definite volume + no definite shape (particles close but can slide past each other). Gas: no definite shape + no definite volume (particles spread to fill container).
Q37 · Solubility & Temperature
F. Increases
As temperature increases, solubility of a solid solute in water...
CORRECT ANSWER
F. Increases (for most solid solutes)
Higher temperature → faster-moving water molecules → can pull more solid solute into solution. IMPORTANT EXCEPTION to mention: solubility of gases in liquids DECREASES with temperature (warm soda goes flat faster).
