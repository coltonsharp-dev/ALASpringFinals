(a) List at least three organelles found in BOTH plant and animal cells, and briefly describe the job of each one.

(b) List at least two structures found ONLY in plant cells and explain why a plant needs each one.

(c) Animal cells often look round or irregular while plant cells look more like rectangles or boxes. Explain why plant cells have this shape.
Question 77FRQ · Body Systems
Describe how the respiratory system and the circulatory system work TOGETHER to deliver oxygen from the air all the way to the cells in your body, and to remove carbon dioxide.

In your answer, you must use and correctly explain at least five of these terms: lungs, alveoli, diaphragm, red blood cells, hemoglobin, capillaries, heart, oxygen, carbon dioxide, mitochondria.

Step-by-step is fine — pretend you are tracing one molecule of oxygen from the air to a cell.
Question 78FRQ · Plate Tectonics
Compare the three main types of plate boundaries: convergent, divergent, and transform.

For each of the three boundary types, your answer must include:
(a) what the plates are doing (their motion),
(b) one geological feature or event that forms there (e.g., a mountain range, mid-ocean ridge, volcano, earthquake), and
(c) one real-world example (a place on Earth).
Question 79FRQ · Weather & Biomes
The tropical rainforest biome has very high biodiversity, while the desert biome has much lower biodiversity.

(a) Identify at least three abiotic factors (non-living parts of the environment) that explain why the rainforest can support so many species.

(b) Identify at least two abiotic factors that make the desert a much harsher environment for life.

(c) Describe two adaptations — one of an organism in the rainforest, and one of an organism in the desert — and explain how each adaptation helps that organism survive.
